About me

I am a Ph.D. student at the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science and Engineering, University of Washington. My board interests lie in high performance computing and deep learning.

I am advised by Luis Ceze in the Sampa computer architecture group. I received my B.A.Sc. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Toronto in 2012, and my M.S. in Computer Science and Engineering from the University of Washington in 2015.

My work is generously funded by various sources including the Weil Family Endowed Fellowship, a Qualcomm Innovation Fellowship, the National Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada, and the Center for Future Architectures Research.
